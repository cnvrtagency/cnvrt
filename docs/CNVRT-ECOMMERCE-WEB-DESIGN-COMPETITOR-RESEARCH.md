# CNVRT Ecommerce Web Design competitor and service-page research

Updated: 16 July 2026  
Status: Approved working direction for `/services/ecommerce-web-design/`

## Executive decision

Ecommerce Web Design should be a separate commercial page because it owns a different buyer problem from Web Design and Shopify.

- Web Design owns broader website strategy, messaging, lead generation and responsive website delivery.
- Ecommerce Web Design owns product discovery, catalogue architecture, merchandising, collection and product-page UX, mobile shopping, trust and conversion journeys.
- Shopify owns platform-specific theme development, apps, integrations and implementation.
- Ecommerce SEO owns organic catalogue visibility, technical search control and search-focused product or collection content.

The page should stay platform-aware but platform-neutral. It may form one engagement with Shopify delivery, but it must not imply that theme development, migration, product-data preparation or custom engineering are automatically included.

## Research method and limits

The competitor set was selected for UK relevance, ecommerce design depth and commercial overlap. It is not presented as an unsupported ranking by revenue, traffic or staff count. Public service pages and current search results were reviewed on 16 July 2026.

Competitor claims are recorded as claims made on their own sites, not independently verified performance facts.

## Official and primary research findings

### Product discovery and filtering

Baymard's current product-list research covers how customers scan, filter, sort and compare products. It reinforces that ecommerce design must include product-list information, category structure, filter logic, applied-filter states, sorting and mobile interaction rather than treating collection pages as a simple image grid.

- [Baymard Product Lists and Filtering UX](https://baymard.com/research/ecommerce-product-lists)
- [Baymard applied-filter research](https://baymard.com/blog/how-to-design-applied-filters)
- [Baymard product-list information research](https://baymard.com/blog/product-listing-information)

### Mobile shopping

Baymard's direct-to-consumer audit scope covers mobile navigation, search, product lists, filtering, product images, descriptions, cart and checkout. The CNVRT page should therefore make mobile behaviour part of every template decision rather than a final responsive adaptation.

- [Baymard Direct-to-Consumer Ecommerce UX Audit](https://baymard.com/audits/direct-to-consumer)

### Accessibility

W3C guidance supports consistent navigation, multiple ways to find content, meaningful structure, keyboard access, labelled controls, clear instructions and understandable error handling. Ecommerce design must include these requirements during wireframing and validation, while acknowledging that final compliance also depends on implementation and ongoing content.

- [W3C accessibility principles](https://www.w3.org/WAI/fundamentals/accessibility-principles/)
- [W3C forms tutorial](https://www.w3.org/WAI/tutorials/forms/)
- [WCAG overview](https://www.w3.org/WAI/standards-guidelines/wcag/)

### Performance and search presentation

Core Web Vitals use LCP, INP and CLS as stable field measures. Ecommerce design should account for image strategy, interaction complexity, layout stability and implementation weight without promising a score that depends on the final build, apps and content.

Google's product documentation also shows that product pages, variants, availability, price, delivery, returns and product imagery have consequences beyond the visible interface. Design, catalogue data, structured data and Merchant Center feeds need consistent source information.

- [web.dev Core Web Vitals](https://web.dev/articles/vitals)
- [Google product structured data](https://developers.google.com/search/docs/appearance/structured-data/product)
- [Google merchant listing structured data](https://developers.google.com/search/docs/appearance/structured-data/merchant-listing)

## Market strengths CNVRT should match

- Visible ecommerce and product-brand proof.
- A design method that moves through discovery, journeys, wireframes, high-fidelity design and prototyping.
- Clear product and collection template coverage.
- Mobile shopping treated as a first-order design problem.
- Storefront design connected to platform delivery and continued improvement.
- A practical explanation of whether the work is a new store, redesign, focused template project or retained optimisation programme.
- Clear ownership, handover and implementation responsibilities.

## Common market weaknesses CNVRT can avoid

- Using `high-converting` as an unsupported adjective or guaranteeing a conversion uplift from a redesign.
- Treating ecommerce design and Shopify development as the same service.
- Listing homepage, collection, product and cart templates without explaining the customer decisions each must support.
- Presenting checkout as fully customisable when the platform, plan and payment provider may restrict it.
- Treating mobile as a smaller desktop layout.
- Promising speed or Core Web Vitals scores before apps, scripts, media and implementation are known.
- Bundling product copy, photography, data enrichment and bulk catalogue preparation into an undefined content promise.
- Reporting only conversion rate without considering product discovery, add-to-cart behaviour, order value, customer quality or attribution limits.
- Using awards and partner badges as the main evidence of design quality.

## Approved CNVRT service boundary

### Core service

- Customer, competitor and catalogue research.
- Existing analytics, search behaviour and customer-question review where data is available.
- Catalogue architecture, navigation and customer-journey mapping.
- Collection, product-list, search, filtering and sorting UX.
- Product-page, cart and available checkout-handoff design.
- Responsive wireframes, visual design and proportionate interaction prototypes.
- Content, product-data and asset requirements.
- Accessibility and performance considerations during design.
- Measurement requirements and a prioritised post-launch improvement roadmap.
- Implementation-ready handover or connected delivery through the appropriate build service.

### Separately assessed and scoped

- Shopify or other platform implementation.
- Theme development, migration and custom engineering.
- Platform, app and payment-provider fees.
- Bulk product import, catalogue cleansing, data enrichment and merchandising setup.
- Product photography, video, custom illustration and extensive product copy.
- ERP, PIM, warehouse, subscription or other complex integrations.
- Ongoing CRO research, testing and implementation.
- Legal, tax, payment, accessibility and international-commerce advice requiring a qualified specialist.

### Safeguards

- No guaranteed conversion rate, revenue uplift, speed score or search position.
- Checkout control is described according to the selected platform and plan.
- Client platforms, analytics and customer data remain in client-controlled accounts.
- Research and source-file ownership are agreed in scope.
- Final accessibility and performance depend on implementation, third-party scripts and ongoing content as well as design.

## Page decisions

- Target `ecommerce web design agency` naturally in metadata, eyebrow and page copy.
- Lead with how people discover, compare and buy rather than platform features.
- Use Heatons as the primary qualitative proof and connect UberKinky and Ya Omri as supporting catalogue and brand examples.
- Keep all performance claims qualitative until the underlying project evidence and client approval are complete.
- Use equal-depth outcome and process cards to preserve the established service-page rhythm.
- Link to Shopify, Ecommerce SEO and Email Marketing as the next logical parts of the customer system.
- Add the route to the Services directory immediately. Review its homepage row during the separately approved homepage copy pass.
