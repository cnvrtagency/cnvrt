# CNVRT AEO and AI search service research

Research date: 16 July 2026  
Status: Current working brief for `/services/aeo/`  
Scope: Official search-platform guidance and ten UK agency service propositions

## 1. Executive decision

CNVRT should keep AEO as a distinct commercial page because the buyer problem and monitoring requirement are distinct: a business wants to know how it is represented, mentioned and cited in AI-generated answers.

The delivery should not be positioned as a separate replacement for SEO. The credible offer is a joined-up programme covering:

1. A documented AI visibility baseline.
2. Commercial question and prompt research.
3. Entity and factual consistency.
4. Useful answer-led content supported by original expertise.
5. Technical accessibility and supported structured data.
6. Credible off-site references and third-party validation where appropriate.
7. Repeat monitoring connected to referral traffic and qualified actions.

The differentiator is not another acronym. It is a transparent method, implementation capability and evidence discipline.

## 2. What the platforms actually say

### Google Search

Google states that its established SEO best practices remain relevant to AI Overviews and AI Mode. It says there are no additional technical requirements, no special schema and no new AI text file required for inclusion.

Google also states that:

- a page must be indexed and eligible to appear in Search with a snippet;
- important content should be available in text;
- internal links and page experience continue to matter;
- structured data must match visible content;
- Merchant Center and Business Profile information should remain current;
- inclusion is not guaranteed;
- AI-feature performance is included within the Search Console Web performance data rather than supplied as a separate complete report.

Source: [Google Search Central, AI features and your website](https://developers.google.com/search/docs/appearance/ai-features)

### Microsoft Bing and Copilot

Bing introduced AI Performance in Bing Webmaster Tools in public preview in February 2026. The available measurements include total citations, average cited pages, sampled grounding queries, page-level citation activity and trends over time.

Bing explicitly cautions that citation counts do not indicate placement, authority, ranking or the role of a page in an individual answer. This makes the data useful but not a single performance score.

Source: [Bing Webmaster Blog, Introducing AI Performance](https://blogs.bing.com/webmaster/February-2026/Introducing-AI-Performance-in-Bing-Webmaster-Tools-Public-Preview)

## 4. Recurring competitor strengths

The strongest pages consistently include:

- a direct AI visibility audit or review as the entry point;
- an explanation of mentions, citations and recommendations;
- a named set of AI platforms;
- competitor and prompt benchmarking;
- entity, content, technical and off-site work together;
- ongoing monitoring rather than a one-off content rewrite;
- a clear link back to SEO foundations;
- proof, reporting examples or proprietary research;
- a direct commercial CTA.

## 5. Recurring competitor weaknesses

The audit also found common risks:

- guarantees or language implying an agency controls inclusion;
- treating variable prompt outputs as stable rankings;
- presenting special schema, files or formatting as a universal shortcut;
- vague claims about how AI systems work internally;
- reporting citations without traffic, lead or revenue context;
- broad AI marketing language that loses the actual buyer problem;
- service lists without a repeatable baseline and retest method;
- proof figures whose definitions are difficult to assess from the page.

## 6. Approved CNVRT service boundary

### Included where relevant

- AI visibility and citation baseline.
- Agreed prompt set by platform, date and UK market.
- Competitor and cited-source review.
- Entity and factual-consistency audit.
- Research, comparison and decision-stage question mapping.
- Priority page and content restructuring.
- Original expertise, evidence and source development.
- Crawlability, indexation, rendering and supported schema review.
- Internal linking and source-page architecture.
- Review of relevant external validation and authority gaps.
- Repeat prompt monitoring.
- Bing Webmaster Tools AI Performance review where available.
- AI referral, landing-page and conversion analysis.

### Excluded or separately scoped

- Guaranteed mentions, citations, recommendations or rankings.
- Manipulation of model training data.
- Fabricated reviews, sources, authors or third-party validation.
- Mass-produced filler pages created only to target prompt variants.
- Unsupported schema or files presented as a ranking requirement.
- Paid placements, digital PR campaigns or large research studies unless scoped.
- A universal AI visibility score presented as exact or complete.

## 7. Monitoring method

The following method is approved for the page and proposals:

1. Agree the business questions, platforms, UK market context and competitors.
2. Record the prompt wording, platform, available mode or model detail, date and observable response.
3. Capture brand mentions, linked citations, cited pages, competitors and recurring source types.
4. Separate navigational prompts from research, comparison and recommendation prompts.
5. Review Bing AI Performance citations, cited pages and grounding queries where access and coverage exist.
6. Review GA4 or equivalent referral sources, landing pages, engagement and qualified conversions.
7. Use Search Console for overall Web search performance while acknowledging that Google does not provide a complete separate AI-feature breakdown.
8. Repeat the agreed test set on a planned cadence.
9. Treat third-party prompt tracking and manual tests as directional because answers vary.
10. Prioritise work using commercial relevance, source gaps and downstream outcomes rather than citation count alone.

## 8. Page decisions implemented

- Primary intent changed to `AEO agency UK` and `AI search optimisation services`.
- Hero now explains the buyer outcome without guaranteeing inclusion.
- The commercial problem now covers omission and inaccurate representation.
- Outcomes use longer, equally weighted copy focused on baseline, representation, sources and outcomes.
- Service scope now includes a concrete monitoring method.
- Google-specific FAQ wording rejects special AI schema and `llms.txt` as universal requirements.
- Hear Better remains the relevant project, but the page explicitly withholds any AEO performance claim until citation evidence is documented.
- Related services are now SEO Strategy, Technical SEO and Web Design, each tied to an actual AEO constraint.
