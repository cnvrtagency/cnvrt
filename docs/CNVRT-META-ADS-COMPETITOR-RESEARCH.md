# CNVRT Meta Ads service research

Research date: 16 July 2026  
Status: Current working brief for `/services/meta-ads/`  
Scope: Official Meta guidance and ten high-visibility UK paid-social agency propositions

## 1. Executive decision

CNVRT should position Meta Ads as a connected demand-creation system, not as a list of audience-targeting options.

The credible service connects:

1. The offer and reason to act.
2. A repeatable creative strategy and testing rhythm.
3. Campaign architecture and appropriate Meta automation.
4. Pixel, Conversions API and lawful first-party signals.
5. The product page, landing page, form or message journey after the click.
6. Ecommerce revenue, qualified leads or other business outcomes.
7. Clear creative-production boundaries, client account ownership and reporting limitations.

Creative is a core campaign input, but unlimited production is not included automatically. Meta reporting is useful evidence, not complete commercial truth on its own.

## 2. Current Meta platform guidance

### Automation and account structure

Meta's current Advantage+ suite uses automation across audience, placement, budget and creative decisions. Eligible lead campaigns can apply Advantage+ settings by default, and Advantage+ sales is the current name for the former Advantage+ shopping campaign.

CNVRT should use automation when it suits the objective, data and economics. The page should not imply that granular manual audience selection is always the main source of advantage.

Sources:

- [Meta Advantage+](https://www.facebook.com/business/ads/meta-advantage-plus)
- [Meta Advantage+ leads campaigns](https://www.facebook.com/business/ads/meta-advantage-plus/leads)
- [Meta Advantage+ sales campaigns](https://www.facebook.com/business/ads/meta-advantage-plus/sales-campaigns?locale=en_US)

### Sales and lead objectives

Meta positions the Sales objective around website purchases and supports catalogue-led advertising. Lead campaigns can use website forms, instant forms or messaging depending on the customer journey. The objective must match the action the business can measure and follow up properly.

Sources:

- [Meta Sales objective](https://www.facebook.com/business/ads/ad-objectives/sales)
- [Meta lead ads with messaging](https://www.facebook.com/business/ads/ad-objectives/lead-generation/lead-ads-with-messaging)

### Pixel and Conversions API

Meta describes Conversions API as a direct connection between an advertiser's marketing data and Meta's optimisation and measurement systems. It can support website, app, offline and messaging events and is normally strongest when used with the Pixel where appropriate.

Conversions API is not a way to bypass consent, Apple AppTrackingTransparency or European privacy rules. CNVRT should state that clearly and use only lawful, permissioned data.

Sources:

- [Meta Conversions API](https://www.facebook.com/business/help/AboutConversionsAPI)
- [Meta Business Tools](https://www.facebook.com/help/331509497253087/)

### Creative diversification

Meta's current performance guidance emphasises simplified account structure, automation, creative diversification, better data quality and results validation. Stronger creative variation should answer commercial questions across hooks, messages, proof and formats, not simply resize one asset repeatedly.

Sources:

- [Meta Performance 5](https://www.facebook.com/business/ads/performance-marketing?locale=en_GB)
- [Meta ad creative guidance](https://www.facebook.com/business/ads/ad-creative)
- [Meta Advantage+ creative](https://www.facebook.com/business/ads/meta-advantage-plus/creative)

### Budget and evidence

Meta does not publish one universal minimum budget. A useful recommendation depends on the conversion economics, audience, creative capacity and cost of collecting enough evidence to make a decision.

Source: [Meta ad pricing and budgets](https://www.facebook.com/business/ads/pricing)

### Account access and ownership

People should be given appropriate access to ad accounts and business assets. Personal login details should not be shared. CNVRT should work through partner or role access while the client retains ownership of its business portfolio, Page, Instagram profile, ad account, Pixel or dataset and catalogue.

Source: [Meta ad-account roles and permissions](https://www.facebook.com/help/messenger-app/195296697183682/)

### Agency recognition

Meta's public agency route currently centres on the Meta Performance Accelerator for agencies and the Agency Success Center. Eligibility and current status must be verified inside the relevant Meta account before CNVRT uses any badge or partner claim.

Source: [Meta for agencies](https://www.facebook.com/business/agencies/about)

## 4. Recurring competitor strengths

- Creative strategy is treated as a standing operating system, not a one-off launch task.
- High-volume production, UGC, studio work and creator networks are clear differentiators for specialist agencies.
- Strong pages connect first-party data, Pixel, Conversions API and customer-quality feedback.
- Ecommerce propositions use catalogue campaigns, product data and post-click conversion work.
- Better agencies distinguish revenue or qualified leads from likes, clicks and cheap form submissions.
- Meta recognition, named case studies and clear reporting improve trust where the claims are current and verified.
- Some agencies publish minimum budgets, retainers or a direct suitability verdict.
- Cross-channel agencies explain how paid social creates demand that search, email and the website help convert.

## 5. Recurring competitor weaknesses

- Vague claims about data-driven targeting despite Meta's increasing automation.
- Promises of scaling without defining offer quality, economics, creative capacity or conversion volume.
- Creative production described as part of management without a usable allowance or boundary.
- Platform-reported ROAS presented as complete commercial truth without attribution limits.
- Little explanation of account ownership, access, billing and client data.
- Limited privacy and consent language around Pixel, CAPI and retargeting.
- Long lists of placements and formats that do not help a buyer decide what should actually run.
- Weak explanation of the landing page, follow-up, CRM or retention journey after the click.

## 6. Approved CNVRT service boundary

### Core Meta Ads management

- Account, access, history and offer audit.
- Audience problem, customer journey and economics review.
- Campaign objective, architecture, budget and Advantage+ decisions.
- Pixel, Conversions API, event and consent review.
- Facebook and Instagram campaign creation and management.
- Creative strategy, hooks, copy, briefs and useful variants from approved source assets.
- Website sales, catalogue, website lead, instant form or messaging campaigns where appropriate.
- Landing-page, product-page, form and follow-up recommendations.
- Ongoing budget, creative and customer-quality review.
- Commercial reporting with attribution limitations stated clearly.

### Conditional or separately scoped

- New photography or product shoots.
- Creator sourcing, contracting, briefing and usage rights.
- UGC production, product video, motion graphics, 3D and substantial editing.
- High-volume, always-on creative production.
- Catalogue remediation or large product-data projects.
- New landing-page, product-page or form design and development.
- CRM, offline event or substantial server-side integrations.
- Paid-social platforms outside Meta.
- Translation, localisation, licensing and regulated-category review.

### Client responsibilities

- Own the Meta business portfolio and connected assets.
- Provide approved source assets, brand rules and accurate product or service claims.
- Confirm rights to use imagery, customer content, creator material and music.
- Provide prompt creative and compliance approvals.
- Provide lawful ecommerce, CRM or lead-quality data when it is expected to influence optimisation.
- Maintain suitable fulfilment, customer service and sales follow-up.

### Not promised

- Unlimited creative production.
- A guaranteed number of sales, leads, ROAS or cost per acquisition.
- Guaranteed ad, account or product approval.
- A Meta badge or partner claim before eligibility is verified.
- Retargeting or server-side tracking that bypasses consent or privacy requirements.
- Scaling spend before the offer, creative, journey and economics support it.
- Ownership of client assets through a CNVRT-controlled personal account.

## 7. Page decisions implemented

- Primary search role changed to `Meta Ads agency Manchester`, supported by Facebook and Instagram Ads language.
- The hero now leads with creative earning the next action rather than generic returns language.
- Meta is framed as demand creation that depends on the offer, creative, destination and measurement signals.
- Advantage+ is covered as a conditional tool rather than a promise or substitute for strategy.
- Pixel and Conversions API are connected to consent, lead quality and commercial reporting.
- The outcomes use four equally weighted 22-word descriptions.
- Creative direction and variants from approved assets are included, while shoots, creators, substantial editing and high-volume production are separately scoped.
- Client asset ownership and role-based access are explained in the FAQ.
- Heatons replaces Hear Better as the relevant proof project, without repeating the unapproved 9.6x performance figure on the service page.
- Related services now connect paid-social learning to Shopify, landing pages and retention rather than repeating a generic service list.
